<span>Six ways to support women's health</span>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--copy-block paragraph--view-mode--full">
          
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p>In May, many of us celebrate the women in our lives. In addition to Mother’s Day on May 11, May marks <a href="https://nationaltoday.com/womens-health-month/"><span>National Women’s Health Month</span></a>.&nbsp;</p><p>Women face distinct challenges and opportunities when it comes to health. Some conditions only impact women, while other conditions disproportionally affect women when compared to the general population.</p><p><span>Focusing on improving women’s health doesn’t just yield personal and professional benefits, such as better health outcomes or reduced absenteeism, it’s also smart economics. For every $1 invested in women’s health, a&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.mckinsey.com/mhi/our-insights/closing-the-womens-health-gap-a-1-trillion-dollar-opportunity-to-improve-lives-and-economies"><span>$3 return on economic growth</span></a><span> can be seen.</span></p><h2><strong>Key health concerns for women</strong></h2><p>Key <a href="https://www.uchealth.com/en/media-room/articles/the-top-5-womens-health-concerns-for-womens-health-day"><span>health issues</span> for women</a> include breast, ovarian and cervical cancers; infertility; <a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/three-pillars-good-employee-mental-health">mental health</a> conditions such as depression and anxiety; Alzheimer’s disease; and heart disease, which is also the overall <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/heart-disease/data-research/facts-stats/index.html#:~:text=Heart%20disease%20is%20the%20leading,seconds%20from%20cardiovascular%20disease.1">leading cause of mortality</a> for adults in the United States.</p><p>May, coincidentally, is also <a href="https://www.bonehealthandosteoporosis.org/awareness-month/#:~:text=May%20is%20Osteoporosis%20Awareness%20and%20Prevention%20Month!">National Osteoporosis Awareness Month</a>. Although not exclusively a women’s health issue, osteoporosis, characterized by weakened, fragile bone, disproportionally impacts women.&nbsp;</p><p><span>Worldwide,&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.osteoporosis.foundation/health-professionals/fragility-fractures/epidemiology">osteoporosis affects 33% of women</a><span> 51 and older in contrast to 20% of men in the same age group</span>. Part of the reason for the 13% difference is hormonal.</p><p>Because estrogen stimulates bone building, the decline of estrogen during perimenopause and significantly <a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8836058/">more during menopause</a>, can lead to reduced bone density.</p><h2><span><strong>Six ways to support women's health</strong></span></h2><p>Adopting healthful lifestyle practices, including eating a nutrient-rich diet, exercising, <a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/how-help-reduce-employee-stress-and-improve-their-physical-and-mental-health">managing stress</a> and getting adequate sleep are essential for promoting an individual's health throughout every stage of life. Let’s take a closer look at six key considerations and practical tips for maintaining women's health you can incorporate into your wellness programs.</p></div>
      
      </div>

            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><strong>A good wellness program should empower the women in your workforce to:</strong></p><ol><li><p><strong>Get regular screenings and exams. </strong><span>Although </span><a href="http://www.ucsfhealth.org/-/media/project/ucsf/ucsf-health/pdf/womens_health_passport_chart.pdf"><span>recommendations</span></a><span> may vary according to family history and individual risk factors, getting regular wellness visits, physicals and </span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/countonyou#screenings"><span>health screenings</span></a><span> for cancer and other conditions should be a part of a preventive care plan. They can identify health issues early for more effective treatment.</span>&nbsp;</p><p>&nbsp;</p></li><li><p><strong>Eat a varied, healthy diet. </strong><span>Women typically have lower energy (calorie) needs, which means less food. This can make it more challenging to achieve an adequate intake of all essential </span><a href="https://lpi.oregonstate.edu/sites/lpi.oregonstate.edu/files/pdf/mic/micronutrients_for_health.pdf"><span>vitamins and minerals</span></a><span>. </span><a href="https://womenshealth.gov/healthy-eating"><span>Eating well</span></a><span> includes getting calcium and vitamin D (critical for bone health and making new skin cells), potassium, iron, folate and vitamin B12. Other key nutrients for women include magnesium; omega-3 fatty acids, which can be found in seafood, seeds, oils and nuts; vitamin C; and choline. Getting these nutrients can help maintain a healthy weight and avoid preventable diseases.</span></p><p>&nbsp;</p></li><li><p><strong>Exercise. </strong>The <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db443.htm">Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reports</a> only 20% of women over age 18 meet the <a href="https://odphp.health.gov/our-work/nutrition-physical-activity/physical-activity-guidelines"><span>physical activity guidelines for Americans</span></a> for aerobic exercise and strength training. This percentage also decreases with age. Two major benefits of exercise include weight management and positive bone health (for weight-bearing exercise). Pressed for time? Here are <a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/13-ways-maintain-wellness-during-holidays">four ways to exercise</a> even when a schedule is packed.</p><p>&nbsp;</p></li><li><p><strong>Drink less. </strong><a href="https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/publications/brochures-and-fact-sheets/women-and-alcohol"><span>Limiting alcohol intake</span></a><span> can improve health and strengthen liver, heart and cognitive function, while also decreasing the risk of breast cancer. Limiting alcohol to one drink, or less, per day for</span><em><span><strong>&nbsp;</strong></span></em><span>women can help with maintaining a healthy weight, improving cardiovascular health and reducing risk of cancer and diabetes.</span></p><p>&nbsp;</p></li><li><p><strong>Avoid smoking. </strong><span>Avoiding smoking </span><a href="https://www.lung.org/quit-smoking/smoking-facts/impact-of-tobacco-use/women-and-tobacco-use"><span>can help women</span></a><span> reduce the risk of many types of cancers, </span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/seven-tips-reduce-heart-disease-risk"><span>heart disease</span></a><span> and gum disease, among other conditions. Learn more about </span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/how-your-employees-can-quit-smoking-and-help-reduce-copd-risk?utm_source=eloqua&amp;utm_medium=email&amp;utm_campaign=dx_org_les_202411_Coaching&amp;utm_content=&amp;utm_term=&amp;utm_marketing_tactic="><span>quitting smoking and reducing chronic obstructive pulmonary disease risk</span></a><span> today.</span></p><p>&nbsp;</p></li><li><strong>Focus on mental health. </strong>Some <a href="https://www.psychiatry.org/getmedia/aa325a61-5b60-4c71-80f1-dc80cf83c383/Mental-Health-Facts-for-Women.pdf"><span>mental health</span></a> conditions, such as depression and anxiety disorders, are more common in women. Addressing mental health can lead to a better diet and physical health, more controlled stress and less substance abuse. Take steps to promote positive mental health throughout every <a href="https://womenshealth.gov/mental-health/good-mental-health/good-mental-health-every-age"><span>stage of life</span></a>, which can include journaling to help provide perspective and relieve future stress or talking to a mental health professional.</li></ol></div>
