<span>Lab data as the missing link for more effective chronic disease management; a value-based care approach</span>
<div class="paragraph paragraph--type--copy-block paragraph--view-mode--full">
          
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><span>Chronic diseases are rapidly increasing in the United States, placing a growing burden on healthcare providers, patients and payers participating in&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/organizations/organizations-we-serve/value-based"><span>value-based care</span></a><span> models. Managing these conditions effectively requires proactive care and a strategic approach to data-driven decision-making.</span></p></div>
      
      </div>
  <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--copy-block paragraph--view-mode--full">
          
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><span>These illnesses not only diminish patients' quality of life, but they also contribute to escalating healthcare costs.</span></p><p><span>Value-based care organizations must prioritize these and other chronic conditions taking into account their patient populations. Successfully managing these conditions requires proactive, data-driven strategies that support targeted interventions, lifestyle modifications and preventive measures.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Access to actionable data is the foundation of this approach, helping organizations improve health outcomes while reducing costs.</span></p><p><a><span><strong>Data-driven patient identification for value-based care success</strong></span></a></p><p><span>Identifying high- and rising-risk patients is critical in value-based care, and lab data also plays a central role in this process. By integrating diverse data sources, organizations can create a more complete patient risk profile:</span></p></div>
      
      </div>
<blockquote>
  <span class="quote"><p><a><span>In 2023, Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) in the Medicare Shared Savings Program (MSSP) achieved over&nbsp;</span></a><a href="https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/press-releases/medicare-shared-savings-program-continues-deliver-meaningful-savings-and-high-quality-health-care"><span>$2.1 billion in net&nbsp;savings</span></a><span>&nbsp;—the highest in program history—by leveraging data to enhance chronic care management.</span></p></span>

          </cite>
</blockquote>

  <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--copy-block paragraph--view-mode--full">
          
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><span><strong>The role lab data plays in chronic disease management</strong></span></p><p><span>Lab data plays a crucial role in this transformation, helping organizations identify at-risk patients earlier, monitor disease progression and implement targeted interventions. Many organizations have clinical resources and programs, but real success comes from knowing when and where to deploy them.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>By integrating lab insights with other data sources, value-based care organizations can make sure their investment in care management and clinical innovation results in improved health and value-based care performance.</span></p><p><span><strong>The </strong></span><a><span><strong>growing challenge of chronic disease in value-based care</strong></span></a></p><p><span>Since 1995, the number of Americans with at least one chronic disease has grown from 118 million to 129 million, with projections </span><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/pcd/issues/2018/18_0151.htm#:~:text=Heart%20disease%20has%20been%20the,cause%20of%20death%20since%201933."><span>reaching 171 million by 2050</span></a><span>.&nbsp;This increase places a significant burden on healthcare systems and increases the urgency for data-driven management strategies.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Among the most prevalent and costly conditions are&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/leveraging-lab-data-combat-diabetes-and-chronic-kidney-disease"><span>diabetes, chronic kidney disease</span></a><span> (CKD) and&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/education-events/articles/three-healthy-habits-fight-cardiovascular-disease"><span>cardiovascular disease</span></a>.</p></div>
      
      </div>
<div class="callout  default">

            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><ul><li><span><strong>Electronic medical record (EMR) data:</strong> Provides a patient’s medical history, past diagnoses and physician interactions</span></li><li><span><strong>Claims data:</strong> Highlights utilization trends, including hospitalizations and medication adherence</span></li><li><span><strong>Social determinants (</strong></span><a href="https://www.nrpa.org/publications-research/data-and-mapping-resource-library/area-deprivation-index/#:~:text=The%20Area%20Deprivation%20Index%20is,education%2C%20employment%20and%20housing%20quality."><span><strong>Area Deprivation Index</strong></span></a><span><strong>):</strong> Uncovers hidden disparities by assessing income, housing stability and access to care</span></li><li><span><strong>Lab data:</strong> Essential for tracking disease progression and early risk detection</span></li><li><span><strong>Patient-reported data:</strong> Captures symptoms, lifestyle factors and barriers to care</span></li></ul></div>
      
      </div>
</div>
          </div>
  
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
  <div class="paragraph paragraph--type--copy-block paragraph--view-mode--full">
          
            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-copy field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field__item"><p><span>Lab data is particularly valuable because it provides objective, real-time insights into a patient’s health and often includes information that’s missing in claims data.</span></p><p><span>For value-based care organizations operating across multiple EMRs or without the infrastructure to fully integrate their data warehouse with disparate EMR systems, access to comprehensive clinical data can be limited. In these cases, Labcorp can serve as a single point of integration offering a streamlined alternative to costly and often unsuccessful multi-EMR integrations and helping fill critical gaps in lab values and clinical insight.</span></p><p><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/data/insight-analytics"><span>Labcorp Insight Analytics</span></a><span>™ overlays lab data with geographic health trends, quality metrics and demographic information to help organizations pinpoint at-risk populations and develop targeted intervention strategies.</span></p><p><span><strong>Turning data into action: Targeted interventions for better outcomes</strong></span></p><p><span>Once high-risk patients are identified, data-driven interventions drive timely, personalized care. Lab data helps healthcare organizations proactively engage patients, improve treatment adherence and&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/organizations/capabilities/quality-performance/gaps-in-care"><span>close care gaps</span></a>, <span>creating opportunities for:</span></p><p><span><strong>1. Proactive patient outreach and care team allocation</strong></span><br /><span>Using lab data helps identify at-risk patients before complications arise. Enhanced clinical insights also power better allocation of specific clinical or administrative teams.&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Identifying patients before complications occur allows teams the opportunity for<strong>&nbsp;</strong>early engagement through email and text messages to remind patients to complete recommended screenings and follow-up appointments.</span></p><p><span><strong>2. Personalized care plans</strong></span><br /><span>Lab data allows you to create tailored interventions based on lab results, patient history and social determinants of health. A data-driven approach allows care teams to:</span></p><ul><li><span>Adjust medication and treatment plans based on trending lab values</span></li><li><span>Recommend lifestyle modifications to reduce risk factors</span></li><li><span>Connect patients with care coordinators and support programs</span></li></ul><p><span><strong>3. Improved treatment adherence</strong></span><br /><span>Medication adherence and lifestyle changes are essential for managing chronic conditions, yet many patients struggle with compliance. Lab data enables ACOs to:</span></p><ul><li><span>Monitor patient progress (e.g., track HbA1c levels for diabetes management)</span></li><li><span>Prioritize outreach to patients with changing lab values</span></li><li><span>Help provide timely follow-ups for medication adjustments</span></li></ul><p><span>A Centers for Medicare &amp; Medicaid Services (CMS) study found that ACOs improving chronic disease management received&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.labcorp.com/Medicare%20Shared%20Savings%20Program%20Continues%20to%20Deliver%20Meaningful%20Savings%20and%20High-Quality%20Health%20Care%20%7C%20CMS"><span>$3.1 billion</span></a><span> in performance payments in 2023, illustrating how early intervention leads to better health outcomes and financial success.</span></p></div>
